Student Activities

Our students don't just study the Word — they live it. Through hands-on ministry, they develop pastoral skills and deepen their understanding of Christian service.

At MELBAC, ministry is not just taught in the classroom — it is practised in the community. Our students are actively engaged in three key areas of service that embody the love, compassion, and authority of Jesus Christ.

Prison & Hospital Ministry
01

Outreach Ministry

Prison and Hospital Ministry

Our students participate in prison and hospital visitations to apply their studies in real-world settings. These experiences provide opportunities to share their faith, offer spiritual support, and provide compassionate care to those in need.

These ministries offer valuable hands-on experience, helping students develop their pastoral skills and deepen their understanding of ministry as a calling, not merely a profession.

"The Spirit of the Lord is upon me, because he has anointed me to preach good news to the poor."
— Luke 4:18
Regular prison visitations to share the Gospel and offer prayer
Hospital ward visits offering comfort, prayer, and scripture reading
Pastoral skills development in challenging real-world environments
02

Compassionate Service

Widows and Orphanage Care

Students actively engage in Widows and Orphanage Care, embodying the biblical call to serve the vulnerable. This involvement takes many forms, from regular visits to local orphanages and care facilities, where students offer companionship, practical assistance, and spiritual encouragement.

Students also organise events to provide essential resources — food, clothing, and educational materials — demonstrating the tangible love of Christ to those society often overlooks.

"Pure and genuine religion in the sight of God the Father means caring for orphans and widows in their distress."
— James 1:27
Regular visits to orphanages and widows' homes
Resource drives providing food, clothing and educational materials
Spiritual encouragement and scripture-based counseling

Elderly Outreach

Care for the Elderly

Students may provide companionship, read scripture, and offer prayer to elderly individuals in nursing homes or assisted living facilities. They often provide spiritual support to those who are dealing with issues related to the end of life.

Essentially, our students demonstrate Christian love and compassion through practical service and spiritual support — meeting people at the most vulnerable point of their lives with the grace of God.

"Rise in the presence of the aged, show respect for the elderly and revere your God."
— Leviticus 19:32
Regular visits to nursing homes and assisted living facilities
Scripture reading, prayer, and spiritual companionship
End-of-life spiritual support and pastoral counseling
